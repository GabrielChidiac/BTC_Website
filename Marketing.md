## 1. Summary

BTC Today is a daily Bitcoin briefing for busy professionals who own BTC but do not have time to follow it. Free tier is a 3-minute daily text brief. Pro tier adds institutional depth, full archive, PDF, and a 4-minute audio brief (Pillar 2, the primary Pro differentiator since the 2026-04-13 pivot).

First LinkedIn post went live Tuesday 2026-04-21 and produced 10 new subscribers in its first 24 hours. Founding-member count sits around 45 of 100 as of 2026-04-22. Monthly recurring revenue is $0 because founding members pay nothing.

This document is a hypothesis, not a finished plan. Three things are true and worth stating plainly before the call:

1. Ten subscribers from one post is a curiosity signal from an adjacent network. It is not product-market fit with the stated ICP.
2. The LinkedIn audience (EPFL students, young engineers) overlaps maybe 20 to 30 percent with the ICP (doctors, lawyers, wealth advisors, corporate execs). The channel that created the first 10 is probably not the channel that creates the next 100.
3. With N=10 there is no data to optimize. The first job is to run 3 to 5 channel experiments, measure, and kill the losers. Not to scale what appears to be working.

This doc is structured to brief a marketing professional cold and to be updated after the call.

## 2. Target audience (ICP)

### Primary ICP

Busy professionals who already own Bitcoin and do not have a daily routine to keep up with it.

- Typical roles: doctors, lawyers, founders, engineers, corporate managers, wealth advisors.
- Typical context: one to three hours of inbox and admin before the first real meeting of the day. Will read over coffee or listen during a commute.
- Mental model: "I hold BTC because I believe in the long-term thesis. I do not have time to read Bitcoin Magazine, scroll X, or watch podcasts. I want a 3-minute text brief or a 4-minute audio brief and I want to be done."
- What they are not: crypto-native, Twitter-pilled, traders, institutional HNW (different pricing and distribution entirely).

### Secondary ICP (honest)

Tech-adjacent professionals in the founder's first and second-degree LinkedIn network. Engineers, young professionals in finance or consulting, SaaS builders. This audience is cheapest to reach, converts the fastest, and is closest to the primary ICP without overlapping perfectly.

### The gap

The primary ICP does not read founder-story LinkedIn posts from students. They read Axios, the WSJ, curated industry newsletters, and listen to Bloomberg or similar on their commute. Reaching them requires either (a) adjacent-professional referrals cascading into the ICP over months, (b) paid acquisition once LTV is known, or (c) partnerships with trusted intermediaries (BTC-focused financial planners, wealth advisory firms).

For the first 100 subscribers, acknowledge that most will come from the secondary ICP and that is acceptable. For the next 500, a plan to cross into the primary ICP is required.

## 3. Positioning and messaging pillars

Three pillars, each in one sentence.

1. **Ruthless time-respect.** Every text brief finishes in under 3 minutes. Every audio brief finishes in under 4 minutes. This is the product promise, enforced at the briefing-generation layer.
2. **Institutional lens, not crypto-native noise.** Framing is about where money flows, macro implications, and long-term value. No hype, no moonboy voice, no tribal language.
3. **AI-curated so the reader does not have to scroll.** One place. No tabs, no X, no podcasts, no newsletters. Read or listen and move on.

### One-line pitch

The daily Bitcoin brief for people who own BTC and have a job. Read in 3 minutes, listen in 4. No hype, no scrolling.

### Taglines to test

- "Everything you need to be current on Bitcoin before your first meeting."
- "BTC in under 4 minutes. Every morning."
- "For busy people who own Bitcoin."

## 4. Competitive landscape

Short read. One line per competitor. Wedge stated.

- **Swan Signal / Swan newsletter.** Institutional-grade, long-form, Swan-brand-first. Over-serves readers who want a deep weekly analysis. Does not serve the 3-minute daily need.
- **Bitcoin Magazine Pro.** Data-heavy, trader-oriented, subscription-priced. Over-serves traders. Does not serve non-crypto-native professionals.
- **Pomp Letter.** Founder-driven, personality-led, broad macro plus crypto. Fine if you already follow Pomp on Twitter. Does not serve readers who do not want to follow personalities.
- **Generic Bitcoin newsletters (Cointelegraph daily, Decrypt, etc.).** Click-driven, hype-adjacent. Wrong tone for the ICP.
- **Podcasts (What Bitcoin Did, Bankless, Onramp).** 60 to 90 minutes. Valuable but not daily habit material for someone with a full calendar.

**BTC Today wedge:** brevity plus institutional-clean tone plus the audio brief (no other player in this space has a disciplined 4-minute daily audio). The three together, not any one alone.

## 6. Channel strategy

Every channel below is framed as an experiment with a hypothesis, a weekly time cost, a success metric, and a kill criterion. Do not run all of them. Run Tier A. Move to Tier B only after Tier A shows or fails.

### Tier A: start here

#### 6.1 LinkedIn organic

- **Hypothesis:** Weekly founder-narrative posts in the voice of the Apr 21 post produce 5 to 15 subscribers per post from the secondary ICP.
- **Cadence:** One post per Tuesday (consistent slot, scheduled like a product feature).
- **Post formats to rotate:**
  1. Founder narrative (the Apr 21 style).
  2. Product milestone ("100 founding members in X days" or similar).
  3. Subscriber-problem post ("I spoke to 20 of our readers last week. Here is what they actually wanted.").
  4. BTC-macro-take with product CTA at the end (not a hard sell).
  5. Teardown post ("I read 7 Bitcoin newsletters this week. Here is what they get wrong.").
- **Weekly time cost:** 2 to 3 hours (writing, editing, engagement in comments).
- **Success metric:** 8 to 12 posts over 60 days, average 5+ subscribers per post.
- **Kill criteria:** If after 6 posts the average drops below 2 subscribers per post, change format or channel.

#### 6.2 Direct outreach (LinkedIn DM and email)

- **Hypothesis:** Personalized feedback-framed outreach to in-ICP professionals converts cold contacts to subscribers at a materially higher rate than post reach.
- **Volume:** 5 messages per weekday. 25 per week. No more (quality decays fast).
- **Targeting:** LinkedIn Sales Navigator filters by job title (wealth advisor, financial planner, attorney, physician in their 30s or 40s, engineer at a mid-to-large company), plus signal-layer filters (posted about Bitcoin, macro, or personal finance in last 90 days).
- **Message shape:** Short. Introduce yourself in one line. Explain what you built and who it is for. Ask for feedback, not a signup. Send the link only if they bite.
- **Weekly time cost:** 3 to 4 hours.
- **Success metric:** 15 to 25 percent reply rate. 5 to 10 percent conversion to subscriber.
- **Kill criteria:** If reply rate is below 5 percent after 100 messages, the targeting or message is wrong. Rework both.

#### 6.3 Newsletter swaps and shout-outs

- **Hypothesis:** Small-to-mid BTC and personal-finance newsletters (500 to 5000 subscribers) will trade mentions for mutual subscriber growth.
- **Target list to build in week 1:** 10 to 20 candidate newsletters. Intersect "Bitcoin" with "personal finance" and "macro." Ignore anything with a tribal or trader-focused audience.
- **Weekly time cost:** 2 hours (research plus outreach).
- **Success metric:** 2 to 5 swaps completed in 60 days. 20 to 100 subscribers per swap.
- **Kill criteria:** If 20 outreach attempts produce zero swaps, the pitch is wrong or the newsletters do not swap. Switch to paid sponsorships or drop the channel.

### Tier B: test after Tier A produces signal

#### 6.4 X / Twitter

- **Hypothesis:** Daily posts summarizing the day's briefing with a link capture some crypto-adjacent professionals who are not on LinkedIn as heavily.
- **Risk:** Audience is largely crypto-native, which is off-ICP.
- **30-day test:** One post per day, first week of posts written by hand to find format. Track follow and click rates.
- **Kill criteria:** If after 30 days fewer than 20 click-throughs to the site, kill the channel.

#### 6.5 Reddit

- **Caution:** Most crypto subs will crush overt promotion. Value-first posts only.
- **Candidate subs:** r/Bitcoin, r/BitcoinBeginners, r/personalfinance (rarely).
- **Approach:** Write one high-effort comment per week answering a real question and mentioning the brief only when directly relevant.
- **Priority:** Low. Do not invest more than 1 hour per week here.

#### 6.6 Hacker News "Show HN"

- **One-shot.** Write the post carefully: lead with the problem, the origin story, the tech stack (which HN loves), and the product. Expect either top-10 traction (200+ subscribers in a day) or silence.
- **Timing:** After founding-member mechanic has some proof (60+ claimed) so the post has a credible social proof hook.

#### 6.7 Product Hunt

- **One-shot.** Moderate fit for newsletters. Founding-member mechanic plus audio brief is a fresh angle. Prepare launch assets (GIF of the product, hero image, maker comment) before posting.
- **Timing:** Week 4 or later, after consistent LinkedIn activity creates a small following who will upvote.

### Tier C: defer until 200+ subscribers

- **SEO content.** Long-term. Not worth the time investment at this stage.
- **Paid LinkedIn ads.** Wait until LTV is known. Burning cash at $0 MRR is a mistake.
- **Paid X ads.** Same logic.
- **Podcast guest appearances.** Pitch after there is a clean case study (built from 10 to 500 in 90 days, here is how).
- **Partnerships with wealth advisors and BTC-focused planners.** High-leverage but requires social proof and likely some form of revenue share. Park for month 3 or 4.

## 8. Founding-member mechanic

### Current setup

- 100 total seats, permanent free Pro access, defined in `src/lib/constants.ts` (FOUNDING_MEMBER_LIMIT).
- `src/lib/founding.ts` computes `spotsLeft` from active founding subscribers in the database.
- Pricing page shows a progress bar ("X of 100 founding spots claimed").
- Approximately 45 of 100 claimed at the time of the Apr 21 post and the 10 new subscribers after.

### Rules for the mechanic

1. **Do not extend the cap if it fills fast.** Scarcity works because it is real. Moving the cap to 150 or 200 teaches the market that the cap is fake.
2. **When spots reach 20 or fewer**, put the remaining count in every outbound post: "20 founding seats left. After that, $59/year." Urgency based on real scarcity.
3. **When full, pivot the homepage and LinkedIn CTAs to paid.** The paid price is the new hook. The founding story becomes part of the origin story: "The first 100 got it free. They helped shape what this is. For everyone else, it is $59 a year."
4. **Every founding member is a testimonial obligation.** Ask every founding member for feedback at day 14 and day 30. Use their words (with permission) in posts and on the landing page. If the first 100 do not produce at least 15 usable testimonials, the mechanic under-returned.

### Risk acknowledged

The first 100 never pay. That is a deliberate trade: 100 free seats for feedback, testimonials, and the social proof needed to convert subscriber 101 onward. If the first 100 do not produce those outputs, the mechanic was a cost.

## 9. Funnel and conversion metrics

Track weekly, not daily. Daily noise at this scale is not a signal.

### Funnel stages

1. **Impression** (LinkedIn post views, X impressions, DM opens). Source: LinkedIn analytics, X analytics, email tracking.
2. **Profile or link visit.** Source: UTM-tagged links, Vercel analytics.
3. **Landing page visit.** Source: Vercel analytics by referrer.
4. **Signup (any tier).** Source: Supabase `subscribers` table, grouped by UTM source.
5. **Founding member activation vs. free signup.** Source: `subscribers.is_founding_member`.
6. **Week-4 retention** (opened daily digest at least 3 times in week 4). Source: email open tracking in Resend plus Supabase query.
7. **Free to paid Pro conversion.** Source: `subscribers.tier` transitions, Stripe webhook log.

### Weekly dashboard (to be built manually in a spreadsheet for now)

Columns: week number, channel, impressions, clicks, signups, founding signups, retention proxy, paid conversions. Update every Friday in 15 minutes.

### Tools

- Vercel analytics (built in).
- UTM tags on every outbound link (naming convention: `utm_source=linkedin&utm_medium=post&utm_campaign=YYYY-MM-DD-topic`).
- Supabase SQL queries run from the dashboard on Fridays.
- No paid analytics tool at this stage. Posthog or similar can be added at 500+ subscribers.

## 10. Brand voice

Pulled from `CLAUDE.md` lines 16 and 17, `emails/welcome.tsx`, and the Apr 21 LinkedIn post.

### Rules

- Peer-to-peer with a professional adult. Not a crypto Twitter degen. Not a finfluencer.
- Short sentences. Data anchors over adjectives.
- Let the data speak. No hype, no moonboy vocabulary, no "HODL," no rocket emojis, no tribal language.
- No em dashes in any external copy (UI, emails, briefings, marketing). Use periods, colons, commas, or parentheses.
- First-person founder voice for LinkedIn and X. Third-person product voice for the landing page and the briefing.
- If a sentence sounds like it could appear in a VC pitch deck, rewrite it.

### Words and phrases to ban

- "Crushing it," "to the moon," "WAGMI," "NGMI," "HODL gang," "Bitcoin maxi," "stacker," "laser eyes."
- "Revolutionary," "game-changing," "next-gen," "disruptive," "unparalleled," "best-in-class."
- "Hey there," "friend," "buddy," any overfamiliar greeting.

### Words and phrases to keep

- "Briefing," "signal," "noise," "read," "listen," "minutes."
- Plain-English nouns. Specific numbers. Named sources.

## 11. Budget and time allocation

### Money

$0 for the first 60 days. Founding-member mechanic means no revenue until seat 101.

Optional paid experiments after day 60, funded from first paid subscribers, not student loans:

- LinkedIn ads targeted by job title ($200 test budget).
- Newsletter sponsorship ($200 to $500 per placement for a mid-sized BTC or personal-finance newsletter).

No paid spend before 50 paid Pro subscribers provide a rough LTV anchor.

### Time

Propose 40 percent of weekly working hours on distribution until 100 founding members are claimed. After that, drop to 25 to 30 percent once the product compounds on its own through referrals.

Typical week (founding phase):

- 3 hours: LinkedIn post writing and engagement.
- 3 to 4 hours: cold outreach (DMs and emails).
- 2 hours: newsletter swap research and outreach.
- 1 hour: weekly metrics review.
- 1 hour: founding member feedback conversations (day-14 and day-30 check-ins).

Total: about 10 to 11 hours per week on distribution. Block it on the calendar. Do not let polish work eat this budget (see `feedback_polish_over_roi.md`).

## 12. Open questions for the marketing professional

Structure the call around these. Bring this list and take notes directly into Section 13.

1. **Channel-ICP mismatch.** Given the primary ICP is doctors, lawyers, wealth advisors, and corporate execs, is direct outreach the right lead channel? Or is there a better wedge (partnerships with financial planners, a specific Substack or newsletter ecosystem, podcast guesting, something else entirely)?
2. **Founding-member cap.** Is 100 the right number? Would 50 produce sharper scarcity? Would 250 produce a deeper wedge? Is the permanent-free mechanic the right long-term trade or should it be a 12-month free Pro instead?
3. **Signal from 10 subscribers.** How do professionals read signal at this scale? What should I be measuring in the first 30 days to know if this is a real business?
4. **LinkedIn post format decay.** The Apr 21 founder-narrative format converted. Does it repeat across 10 more posts, or does the market tune it out after 2 or 3? What formats should I rotate in?
5. **Paid acquisition.** At what point does paid make sense? What signals should I wait for before spending?
6. **Audio brief as primary wedge.** The 4-minute audio brief is positioned as the Pro differentiator. Is that enough to convert free to paid, or does Pro need another pillar (PDF, archive, exclusive content)?
7. **Referral loop.** Currently there is no founding-member-brings-a-friend mechanic. Should there be? If yes, what (swag, lifetime keyring, early-feature access)?
8. **Copy on the landing page.** Does the homepage convert at a reasonable rate given current traffic? Is the ProTeaser gate too aggressive or not aggressive enough?

## 14. Appendix: Apr 21, 2026 LinkedIn post (archived verbatim)

This post produced 10 subscribers in its first 24 hours. Keep it for reference when writing future posts.

> A few months ago I got frustrated trying to keep up with Bitcoin.
>
> Every morning I'd open a handful of tabs, scroll through two newsletters, check X, look at the price, and half an hour later realize I still couldn't answer one simple question: what actually mattered in Bitcoin today?
>
> The newsletters and podcasts out there are fine. But they all assume I have more than 30 minutes a day.
>
> I don't. I want to be up to speed on Bitcoin in under 5 minutes while I'm having coffee, and then move on with my day.
>
> So I built a small site for myself that pulled the day's news into one place. Just for me.
>
> Then I realized a lot of people probably have the same problem and would never build something to solve it.
>
> That's how BTC Today started.
>
> It's a daily Bitcoin brief delivered every morning. No scrolling, no searching, no tabs. Price action, top stories, ETF flows, institutional moves, technical signals, expert insights, and what to watch next. Read in under 6 minutes or listen in under 4.
>
> Everything you need to be current on Bitcoin before your first meeting of the day.
>
> I'm opening the first 100 founding member seats permanently free. 55 are still available. They exist because the people who show up early shape what the product becomes.
>
> If you hold Bitcoin but don't have a dedicated routine for keeping up with it, I'd genuinely appreciate your feedback: btctoday.co
>
> What's the one thing you actually want to know about Bitcoin each day? Reply here. I read everything.

### Why this post worked (hypothesis)

- Founder-narrative frame ("I got frustrated and built a thing").
- Specific problem statement that mirrored the reader's own morning.
- Honest acknowledgment of competition ("the newsletters and podcasts out there are fine").
- Concrete product promise ("under 6 minutes or listen in under 4").
- Genuine CTA that was not a sales pitch ("reply here, I read everything").

Test these elements in future posts independently to isolate which ones drive the signal.
